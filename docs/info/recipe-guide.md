### Ingredients

Most common ingredients used to balance flavor, texture, and sweetness while keeping calories low include various proteins, specialized sweeteners, and stabilizers.

#### Sweetness

*   **SweEX (Erythritol + Xylitol 3:2):** This custom blend is used across most low-calorie recipes, such as *CocoaLow* and *Strawberry Ice Cream*, providing bulk and sweetness with a low glycemic index.
*   **Xylitol:** Frequently used on its own or as part of a blend, it offers sweetness equal to table sugar but with roughly 40% fewer calories.
*   **Flavor Drops (Stevia/Sucralose):** These ultra-sweet liquids allow for "dialing in" final sweetness without adding mass or calories, common in recipes like *Vanilla Protein* and *Cheesecake Light*.
*   **Allulose:** Used as a rare-sugar alternative, it provides a clean sweetness and helps with texture without being counted as "added sugar" in some regions.
*   **Trehalose:** A disaccharide often used in sorbets to add solids and improve texture without making the treat cloyingly sweet.

#### Texture and Body

*   **Protein Powders (Soy Protein Isolate, Whey, Casein):** These are the primary ingredients for boosting protein content while providing structural "body" and creaminess in dairy-free and low-fat bases like *Vanilla Protein* and *Epic Spoons of Nom*.
*   **Vegetable Glycerin (VG):** Crucial for "scoopability," glycerin lowers the freezing point drastically, preventing low-fat bases from freezing into solid, unprocessable blocks.
*   **Inulin:** A prebiotic fiber that mimics the mouthfeel of fat and binds water to improve creaminess in lean recipes like *Lean Coffee* and *Radler Zäpfle*.
*   **Waxy Maize Starch (E1442):** Often used in recipes like *Vegan Vanilla* or *Grapple Sorbet*, this modified starch adds thickness and prevents a watery melt without requiring cooking.
*   **Skim Milk Powder (SMP):** Used in dairy-based "lite" recipes to increase milk solids, which binding free water to prevent icy textures.

#### Flavor

*   **Fresh or Frozen Fruit:** Real fruit like strawberries, cherries, and plums provide the foundational flavor for sorbets and sherbets.
*   **Cocoa Powder (11% or 21%):** Used in chocolate-based recipes like *CocoaLow* and *Toasted Cowcoa*; blooming the cocoa in hot liquid first is recommended to release its full aroma.
*   **Strong Coffee/Instant Espresso:** Provides a robust flavor profile in recipes such as *Espresso Martini Light* and *Lean Coffee*.
*   **Alcohol (Brandy, Vodka, Rum, Amaretto):** Beyond flavor, spirits are used technically to soften the texture and open up aromatic notes.
*   **Natural Extracts and Spices:** Vanilla bean powder, cinnamon, and lemon zest are common "finishing" flavors that add depth without significant caloric impact.
