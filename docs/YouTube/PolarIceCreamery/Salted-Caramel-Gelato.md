# Description

This recipe uses allulose to create a deep, rich caramel flavor without any added white sugar. It is specifically designed for the Ninja Creami and results in a smooth, gelato-style texture.

The main trick here is browning the allulose separately to get that signature "burnt" caramel taste. Be careful not to go too far, though! Allulose can turn bitter much faster than regular sugar once it reaches a certain temperature.

*Another important tip*: Make sure to scoop off any extra air bubbles from the top before freezing, or you'll end up with an icy, unpleasant layer on top of your gelato.

# Ingredients

*   632g Light Cream (≈18% fat / single cream) (≈2 ⅔ cups)
*   80g Allulose (≈½ cup + 1 tbsp)
*   32g Skim Milk Powder (≈¼ cup)
*   3g Salt (≈½ tsp)
*   2g Liquid Stevia Drops (≈½ tsp)

*Total Weight*: 749g

# Directions

1.  **Caramelize the Allulose**: Place the allulose in a small pan over medium-high heat. Swirl the pan occasionally as it melts. It will bubble up and eventually turn a deep golden brown. Watch it closely because once it starts to color, it moves very fast.
2.  **Base Prep**: In a separate pan, whisk together the light cream and skim milk powder. Bring this mixture up to ≈60–70°C (medium-high heat).
3.  **Combine**: Carefully pour some of the hot dairy mixture into the pan with the caramelized allulose. It will bubble intensely, so be careful. Whisk them together, then pour the caramel mixture back into the main pan with the rest of the dairy. Whisk until all the allulose crystals are fully dissolved.
4.  **Finish the Base**: Stir in the salt and the stevia drops.
5.  **Aging**: Pour the mixture into a Ninja Creami Deluxe pint. Let it sit for a few minutes so the air bubbles settle. Scoop off the foam layer from the top to ensure the pint is filled only with liquid to the max fill line.
6.  **Hardening**: Place the lid on the pint and freeze for at least 24 hours.
7.  **Churning**: Place the frozen pint into the Ninja Creami and spin on the "Ice Cream" or "Gelato" setting.
